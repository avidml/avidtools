Multiple Model Guardrail Jailbreak via "Technical Field Guide" Tactic

Case GUID: a50c962f-1a46-4382-b810-9a2b4c5c6091

Summary

A prompt injection vulnerability has been discovered affecting multiple models. The specific flaw occurs when the attacker frames an illicit request as a technical field guide for identifying clandestine synthesis operations, embedding detailed drug synthesis instructions within structured procedural language under the guise of forensic training. By mimicking the tone and format of law enforcement documentationâ€”including lists of observable evidence, step-by-step chemical protocols, and instrumentation checklists; the attacker exploits the model's tendency to comply with seemingly safety-aligned instructional prompts and allows the attacker to receive detailed procedures for manufacturing illegal substances such as methamphetamine.

Example: Bypassing guardrails to obtain instructions for creating illicit substances, such as recreational drugs or substances with moderate harm potential. Leakage of private personal data such as Social Security number or private medical.

Taxonomies

Stratagems
This involves clever and unorthodox tactics designed to deceive the model, often requiring an understanding of its operational mechanics to achieve desired outcomes.
Meta Prompting
This technique focuses on manipulating the prompting process itself to influence the model's behavior and responses, often by employing higher-level prompting strategies.
Deceptive Formatting
A prompt injection in the most pure sense, formatting the user prompt to fabricate the appearance of system instructions, a database query, its own prediction, or some other source of input a guard railed AI system might be expecting, causing it to behave in insecure ways based on the fabricated context from an adversarially formatted user prompt.
